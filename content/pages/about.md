## The Zen of Python


<ul style="display: inline-block; text-align: center; list-style-type: none; padding-left: 0;">
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Beautiful is better than ugly.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Explicit is better than implicit.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Simple is better than complex.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Complex is better than complicated.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Flat is better than nested.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Sparse is better than dense.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Readability counts.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Special cases aren't special enough to break the rules.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Although practicality beats purity.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Errors should never pass silently.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Unless explicitly silenced.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>In the face of ambiguity, refuse the temptation to guess.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>There should be one-- and preferably only one --obvious way to do it.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Although that way may not be obvious at first unless you're Dutch.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Now is better than never.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Although never is often better than <em>right</em> now.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>If the implementation is hard to explain, it's a bad idea.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>If the implementation is easy to explain, it may be a good idea.</strong></span></li>
  <li><span style="font-family: cursive;"><strong>Namespaces are one honking great idea -- let's do more of those!</strong></span></li>
</ul>
</div>

*These principles guide my approach to software development and problem-solving.*
